College trap

Kanishka Shrivastava January 14, 2026 5 min read college-life, coding-joureny, career, learning, mindset

-> College promises a lot.

A good degree.

A secure future.

A clear path to success.

But for many aspiring developers, college slowly becomes a trap — not because it’s useless, but because of what it doesn’t teach.

I realized this a little later than I should have, and maybe that’s exactly why I’m writing this.

-> The Illusion College Creates

!college

In the beginning, everything feels right.

You attend lectures.

You complete assignments.

You memorize syntax.

You clear exams.

You feel productive.

But deep down, something feels off.

You know the theory of Java, but you hesitate before writing real code.

You’ve studied data structures, but struggle to apply them.

You score marks, yet doubt your skills.

College makes you believe that completion equals competence.

It doesn’t.

-> The Real College Trap

!degree

The biggest trap college sets is this:

“If you follow the syllabus, you’ll be industry-ready.”

Most syllabus are outdated.

Most exams reward memory, not thinking.

Most practicals test execution, not understanding.

So students start optimizing for:

Marks instead of mastery

Certificates instead of projects

Passing exams instead of solving problems

Slowly, learning becomes mechanical.

That’s the trap.

-> When Reality Hits

The moment of truth comes when you try to:

Build a real project

Read someone else’s code

Debug an error without a solution online

Apply for internships

Suddenly, grades don’t help.

You realize:

Knowing about code is not the same as knowing how to code.

This realization is uncomfortable.

Sometimes demotivating.

But it’s also powerful.

-> What College Doesn’t Teach You

College rarely teaches:

How to think like a developer

How to break problems into logic

How to read documentation

How to fail, debug, and improve

How to learn independently

These skills aren’t in any syllabus, but they decide your career.

-> Escaping the Trap (What Actually Helped Me)

!working

Escaping the college trap doesn’t mean leaving college.

It means changing how you learn.

Here’s what truly matters:

Build projects, even if they’re messy

Struggle with bugs instead of avoiding them

Read code, not just write it

Learn beyond the syllabus

Marks can get you through semesters, but skills get you through interviews, projects, and real jobs.

Progress feels slow at first, but it’s real.

-> Final Thought

College can give you a degree.

But becoming a developer is something you have to choose every day — through curiosity, practice, and patience.

!laptop

Don’t let college limit you.

Let it be a base, not a boundary.

You don’t need to be perfect.

Just aware, consistent, and willing to grow.
